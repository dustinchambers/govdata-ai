About Civic Value Index

We believe buying a home should be a data-driven decision.

What We Do

Civic Value Index combines four critical datasets that traditionally exist in silos:

  • Crime Data: Real crime statistics, trends, and safety metrics
  • City Services: 311 request response times and service quality
  • Municipal Budget: How your tax dollars are spent in each neighborhood
  • Real Estate Market: Home values, appreciation, and market momentum

Why It Matters

Traditional real estate platforms show you pretty photos and square footage. We show you what really matters:

  • Is this neighborhood getting safer or more dangerous?
  • How quickly does the city respond to service requests?
  • Are you paying premium prices for premium city services?
  • Which neighborhoods are undervalued vs overpriced?

Our Methodology

The Civic Value Index is calculated using:

  • Safety Score (30%): Crime rates, trends, violent vs property crime mix
  • Service Quality (25%): 311 response times, service efficiency
  • Market Performance (25%): Home values, appreciation rates
  • Civic Value Ratio (20%): Proprietary metric measuring service quality per dollar

Data Sources

All data comes from official public sources:

  • Denver Crime Data (2020-2025)
  • Denver 311 Service Request System
  • Denver Checkbook (Transparent Denver Budget Data)
  • Zillow Home Value Index (ZHVI)

Data is updated monthly to ensure accuracy.
